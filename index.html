Good to know

Questions we hear a lot.

What's the difference between pressure washing and soft washing?

Pressure washing uses higher water pressure for tough, durable surfaces like concrete and brick. Soft washing uses low pressure plus cleaning solutions to safely clean siding, roofs and other surfaces that high pressure can damage. We match the method to the material — every time.

How much does it cost?

It depends on the surface, square footage and how much buildup there is. That's why estimates are free and in person — you get a firm, honest number before any work starts. Call or text (314) 229-6210 for the fastest answer.

Will pressure washing damage my siding or roof?

Not when the right method is used. Vinyl siding, shingles and other delicate surfaces get low-pressure soft washing, which kills algae and lifts grime without any damage.

How often should my home's exterior be cleaned?

Most St. Louis homes look their best with a cleaning every 12–24 months. Shaded lots and heavy tree cover grow algae and mildew faster, so those homes may need it more often.
